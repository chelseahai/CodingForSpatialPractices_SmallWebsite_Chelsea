"The mode of human sense perception changes with humanity's entire mode of existence. The manner in which human labor is organized, the machines that extend the hand and the eye, alter not only the work produced but the way we dwell in the world. Technology is not neutral—it bends perception, compresses time, and recasts the aura of life into reproducible fragments."

Walter Benjamin, The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction (1936)
